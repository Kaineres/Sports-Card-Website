# Alt — Features & Pricing

## Overview

Alt is a sports card investment platform focused on graded, authenticated cards. Founded by Leore Avidar. Raised $31M Series A and $75M Series B (notable investors: Naval Ravikant, Tom Brady, Giannis, Kevin Durant, Alexis Ohanian, Stripe's Patrick Collison). Positioned toward premium ($1k+) graded cards. Not fractional ownership — full 100% card ownership only.

Only graded, authenticated cards listed on the exchange. No raw cards.

## Core Products

### Alt Portfolio
Tracks your card collection with daily valuations. Each card's "Alt Value" is updated daily using recent transactions and comparable sales. Single-screen view of all assets, sortable by year, value, change, gain/loss, and purchase date.

### Alt Exchange (Marketplace)
Fixed-price listings and timed auctions for graded cards. Fully searchable by sport, player, year, grade, and card brand. All items verified and authenticated before listing.

### Alt Vault
Physical secure storage facility in Delaware. Cards stored, insured, and verified with high-resolution cameras. Designed for active traders who don't want to ship cards with each transaction.

### Alt Value
Daily card valuations derived from recent comparable transactions and algorithmic "systematic market knowledge." Essentially an automated comp pricing engine.

### Alt Lending (planned)
Card-backed loans using Alt Vault holdings as collateral. Not yet launched as of last source date.

## Fee Structure (as of 2024 — verify current)

| Transaction type | Fee |
|---|---|
| Marketplace (fixed price) seller fee | 9% |
| Auction seller fee — vaulted cards | 7% |
| Auction seller fee — non-vaulted cards | 12.5% |
| Buyer premium at auction | 15% |
| Payment processing (buyer) | 3% |
| Private vault storage | ~$20/card/year |
| Cards listed for sale in vault | Free storage |

## Competitive Position
- Vertically integrated: portfolio tracking + marketplace + physical vault in one platform.
- Targets $1k+ premium/graded cards; underserves the $50–$500 modern-graded beachhead.
- Strong brand ("Alt" = alternative assets) and celebrity investor credibility.
- No decision intelligence layer — analytics exist but no buy/hold/sell synthesis.
- High transaction fees (9–15%) create friction vs. eBay for common-price-point cards.

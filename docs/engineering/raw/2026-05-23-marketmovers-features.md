# Market Movers — Features & Pricing

## Overview

Market Movers is a sports and trading card platform by Sports Card Investor (Geoff Wilson and Tyler Nethercott). Founded 2020. Claims 75,000+ users. Positioned as "the ultimate sports and trading card platform" for both casual collectors and serious investors. Available on mobile and desktop.

## Data Sources

eBay, Fanatics Collect, Goldin, Heritage Auctions, Pristine Auctions, Cards HQ, MySlabs, PSA, SGC, Beckett, CGC. Updates daily.

## Core Features

### Sales History & Price Charts
Tens of millions of sales comps dating back to 2001. Charts and trends including cards "no other app tracks." Price history for cards and sealed boxes.

### Collection Tracking
Track entire collection value in one place. Stats by sport, year, player, set, and grade. Transaction tracking for profit/loss over time. Supports both cards and sealed boxes.

### Card Scanning
Scan graded or raw cards to pull historical sales data instantly.

### Deals
Highlights cards listed below recent comps across partner marketplaces. Designed to surface underpriced buying opportunities.

### Price Alerts
Notifications when a card drops below or spikes above a user-defined price target. Unlimited on top tier; 5 on Starter, 20 on Premium.

### Market Pulse
Macro-level market indexes. Shows big-picture hobby trends. Premium tier and above.

### Intelligence Reports (Unlimited only)
Grade ratios, player ratios, and variation ratios. Described as "the ultimate hobby hack that no other app offers." Designed to surface market inefficiencies before the broader hobby catches on.

## Community / Content Layer
Monthly livestreams with giveaways. Private group video calls with Geoff & T-Pott (Unlimited tier). Weekly newsletter. Active social presence.

## Competitive Position
- Alert-driven UX built for active traders and flippers.
- Strong content/community ecosystem is a retention and acquisition moat.
- Intelligence Reports (grade/player/variation ratios) is the closest thing to analytical signal generation in any incumbent.
- Weakness: no synthesis into explicit buy/sell/hold decisions — data presentation only.

# Card Ladder — Features & Pricing

## Overview

Card Ladder is a sports card analytics and price guide platform. Self-described as building "the industry's only total market index for thousands of players and characters." Tracks every public sale dating back to 2000 — longer historical depth than any competitor they name.

Data sources: eBay, Goldin, Heritage Auctions, Fanatics Collect, and other major marketplaces.

## Core Features

- **Sales history**: 100M+ historical sales going back to 2000. Displays comp data for any card, including cards with no recent sales (uses index modeling to estimate).
- **Card Ladder Index**: Total market index per player/character. Represents fluctuations in a player's sports card market over time.
- **Card Ladder Value**: Proprietary price modeling. Establishes the historical relationship between an individual card and its player index, then uses that relationship to project plausible current price. Fills gaps where no recent sale exists.
- **Pop report integration**: Population reports from PSA, BGS, SGC, and CGC integrated into card detail views.
- **Collection tracking**: Daily value updates for cards in your collection.
- **Advanced filters and sorting**: Multiple sorting algorithms; saved searches. (Pro only.)
- **Price guides**: Baseball, basketball, football, hockey, Pokemon, and more.
- **Daily hobby news and sales recaps**: Included in free tier.
- **Marketplace links**: Buy cards directly from partner marketplaces within the app.

## Pricing

As of February 1, 2025, pricing increased from $15/month ($150/year) to $20/month ($200/year).

| Tier | Price | Key limitations |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | Browse, news, index, marketplace links — no advanced analytics |
| Pro | $20/month or $200/year | Full sales history, advanced filters, saved searches, in-depth analytics |

~$200/year positions them as a premium tool relative to hobbyist-tier free apps.

## Competitive Position

- Pure analytics/lookup tool. No marketplace of their own.
- Strongest in historical data depth and player index modeling.
- Weakness per competitors: lookup-oriented, not decision-oriented. Does not synthesize data into recommendations.

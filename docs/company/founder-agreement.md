# SlabMetrics Founders' Agreement

> Source: `Key Documents/SlabMetrics Founder Agreement.docx`
> Status: Drafted May 2026. Awaiting formal signature. No independent legal counsel has reviewed it — founders acknowledge this and intend to get formal review before incorporating or raising capital.

## Parties

| Founder | Role summary |
|---|---|
| **Kain Kim** | Product, software engineering, technical architecture, infrastructure, data systems, digital marketing (paid acquisition, SEO, content distribution) |
| **Carson Froy** | Card market expertise, brand & social media strategy, field marketing (trade shows, dealer/collector relationships), customer research within the card community |

---

## 1. Equity & Vesting

- **Split:** 50 / 50
- **Vesting period:** 4 years, 1-year cliff
  - Month 0–12: nothing vests
  - Month 12: 25% of each founder's equity vests in one shot (12.5% of total company each)
  - Months 13–48: remaining 75% vests monthly in equal installments
- **Vesting Start Date:** date both founders sign the agreement

### Departure

- Departing founder keeps **vested** equity; all unvested is forfeited
- During the cliff period: departing founder receives **zero equity**
- Remaining founder may repurchase vested equity at fair market value

### Termination for Cause

Trigger conditions: (a) uncured material breach (30-day notice), (b) fraud/embezzlement/intentional misconduct, (c) felony conviction, (d) sustained failure to perform after 30-day written notice and cure opportunity.

---

## 2. Decision-Making

### Operating decisions (single-founder authority)

Each founder can decide within their domain if the decision:
- Is consistent with mission and current plan
- Does **not** commit the business to >$250 without prior consultation
- Is communicated to the other founder in reasonable time

### Major Decisions (unanimous written consent required)

- Entity formation (type, state)
- Any new equity issuance
- Outside capital (debt or equity, any source)
- Sale, merger, acquisition, or dissolution
- Any hire >$1,000/month or >$5,000 total
- Any contract or financial obligation >$500
- Material product pivot (target customer, product category, revenue model)
- Amendment of this agreement
- Profit distribution
- IP licensing, sale, or transfer

### Dispute Resolution

1. Either founder raises disagreement in writing
2. 30-day cooling-off period — no unilateral action during this window
3. If unresolved: optional neutral third-party non-binding recommendation
4. If still unresolved: **status quo prevails** (disputed action does not proceed)

---

## 3. Time Commitment

Both founders are undergraduates at the University of Georgia. No fixed weekly hours. Performance tracked via **quarterly objectives** reviewed at quarter end and documented in a shared record.

Sustained non-performance → dispute resolution → possible termination for cause.

---

## 4. Capital, Expenses & Profits

- **No required capital contribution.** Any voluntary contributions must be documented and signed by both founders.
- **Personal card portfolios are personal property** — including Carson's ~$3k existing portfolio and any future cards purchased for the decision-logging experiment. Gains/losses belong to the individual founder.
- **Reimbursable business expenses:** software, APIs, hosting, marketing, trade shows, travel — reimbursed from revenue before profit distribution.
- **Profit distribution:** proportional to vested equity, by unanimous written consent.
- **Loans:** must be documented (principal, interest, repayment terms); do not affect equity.

---

## 5. Intellectual Property

- All IP created for the Business before or after signing is **assigned to the Business** (and future Company)
- Founders agree to execute any formal IP assignment documents on incorporation
- **Excluded (personal IP):** Kain's separate projects — EdgeIQ, CourtIQ, ONB Washing website, coffee roasting business assets, and any pre-existing work not connected to SlabMetrics

---

## 6. Equity Transfer

- Neither founder may transfer equity to a third party without the other's written consent
- Other founder has **30-day right of first refusal** on any proposed transfer, at the same terms offered to the third party

---

## 7. Confidentiality, Non-Compete, Non-Solicit

| Obligation | Duration after departure |
|---|---|
| Confidentiality (non-public business info) | 2 years |
| Non-compete (direct competitor in card analytics) | 12 months |
| Non-solicit (employees, contractors, customers, partners) | 12 months |

Non-compete **does not** prevent working in the broader sports card industry (dealer, breaker, content) or broader software industry.

- Founders intend to form a **Georgia LLC** (or other mutually agreed entity)
- This agreement's terms will be incorporated into the LLC's operating agreement
- Risk: until LLC is formed, founders have personal liability as a general partnership

# Grading scales

## For future Claude
This page is the authoritative side-by-side reference for the numeric grading scales used by all four major services — PSA (10-point, 2 half-grades), BGS (10-point, half-points at every step, 4 subgrades), SGC (20-step with half-grades), and CGC (20-grade, half-steps, TCG criteria distinguished).
Relevant when: comparing how a specific grade level translates across services, understanding centering tolerances at any grade, or evaluating cross-service equivalence claims.
Key fact: cross-service equivalence claims like "BGS 9.5 ≈ PSA 10" are not supported by the published standards — centering tolerances and defect vocabularies differ meaningfully between services.

## TL;DR

Grade scales are the numeric ranges grading services use to summarize a card's condition. PSA's scale (1–10 with two half-grades at 1.5 and 8.5) is documented except PSA 8.5. BGS's scale (1–10 with half-points at every step, four published subgrades, Pristine Black/Gold label variants) is documented for integer grades plus 9.5 — half-points are interpolations per BGS policy. SGC's scale (1–10 with half-grades at every step plus a separate Pristine 10 above the Gem 10, 20 distinct levels total) is fully documented. CGC's scale (1–Pristine 10, also 20 grades with half-steps, Pristine 10 / Gem Mint 10 two-tier top) is now fully documented. `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]], [[bgs-grading-scale]], [[sgc-grading-scale]], [[cgc-grading-scale]].

## PSA scale

PSA uses a **10-point scale**, **10 = best**. Half-grades exist at **1.5** and **8.5** `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

### PSA 10 — Gem Mint (GEM-MT)

A virtually perfect card `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- Four perfectly sharp corners.
- Sharp focus.
- Full original gloss.
- Free of staining of any kind.
- Slight printing imperfection allowed **only if** it doesn't impair overall appeal.
- Centering tolerance: ≤ ~55/45 percent on the front, ≤ ~75/25 percent on the reverse.

### PSA 9 — Mint

A superb condition card that exhibits **only one** of the following minor flaws `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- A very slight wax stain on reverse.
- A minor printing imperfection.
- Slightly off-white borders.

Centering must be approximately **60/40 or better on the front** and **90/10 or better on the reverse**.

### PSA 8.5

_(half-grade between 8 and 9 — text definition not captured in current ingest; tab not clipped. Stubbed pending follow-up.)_

### PSA 8 — Near Mint-Mint (NM-MT)

A super high-end card that appears Mint 9 at first glance, but on closer inspection may exhibit `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- A very slight wax stain on reverse.
- The slightest fraying at one or two corners.
- A minor printing imperfection.
- Slightly off-white borders.

Centering must be approximately **65/35 or better on the front** and **90/10 or better on the reverse**.

### PSA 7 — Near Mint (NM)

A card with just slight surface wear visible upon close inspection. There may be slight fraying on some corners. Picture focus may be slightly out-of-register. A minor printing blemish is acceptable. Slight wax staining is acceptable on the back of the card only. Most of the original gloss is retained `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

Centering must be approximately **70/30 or better on the front** and **90/10 or better on the back**.

### PSA 6 — Excellent-Mint (EX-MT)

May have visible surface wear or a printing defect that does not detract from overall appeal. A very light scratch may be detected only upon close inspection. Corners may have slightly graduated fraying. Picture focus may be slightly out-of-register. May show some loss of original gloss, may have minor wax stain on reverse, may exhibit very slight notching on edges, and may show some off-whiteness on borders `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

Centering must be **80/20 or better on the front** and **90/10 or better on the reverse**.

### PSA 5 — Excellent (EX)

Very minor rounding of the corners becoming evident. Surface wear or printing defects more visible. Minor chipping possible on edges. Loss of original gloss more apparent. Focus may be slightly out-of-register. Several light scratches may be visible upon close inspection but do not detract from appeal. May show some off-whiteness of borders `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

Centering must be **85/15 or better on the front** and **90/10 or better on the back**.

### PSA 4 — Very Good-Excellent (VG-EX)

Corners may be slightly rounded. Surface wear noticeable but modest. May have light scuffing or light scratches. Some original gloss retained. Borders may be slightly off-white. A light crease may be visible `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

Centering must be **85/15 or better on the front** and **90/10 or better on the back**.

### PSA 3 — Very Good (VG)

Reveals some rounding of corners, though not extreme. Some surface wear apparent, along with possible light scuffing or light scratches. Focus may be somewhat off-register; edges may exhibit noticeable wear. Much, but not all, of the original gloss will be lost. Borders may be somewhat yellowed and/or discolored. A crease may be visible. Printing defects are possible. Slight stain may show on obverse; wax staining on reverse may be more prominent `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

Centering must be **90/10 or better on the front and back**.

### PSA 2 — Good (GOOD)

Corners show accelerated rounding; surface wear starting to become obvious. May have scratching, scuffing, light staining, or chipping of enamel on obverse. There may be several creases. Original gloss may be completely absent. May show considerable discoloration `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

Centering must be **90/10 or better on the front and back**.

### PSA 1.5 — Fair (FR)

Corners show extreme wear, possibly affecting framing of the picture. Surface shows advanced stages of wear: scuffing, scratching, pitting, chipping, staining. Picture possibly quite out-of-register; borders may have become brown and dirty. May have one or more heavy creases `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

To achieve a Fair grade, the card must be **fully intact** — even if heavily worn, the card cannot achieve this grade if it's missing solid pieces (major tears, removal of back layer, missing corner).

Centering must be **approximately 90/10 or better on the front and back**.

### PSA 1 — Poor (PR)

Exhibits many of the same qualities of a PSA 1.5, but defects have advanced to such a serious stage that eye appeal has nearly vanished entirely `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]. A Poor card may:

- Be missing one or two small pieces.
- Exhibit major creasing that nearly breaks through all layers of cardboard.
- Contain extreme discoloration or dirtiness throughout that may make it difficult to identify the issue or content on either the front or back.
- Show noticeable warping or another type of destructive defect.

### Non-grade encapsulation labels

PSA also issues these as labels on the slab, distinct from numeric grades `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- **Authentic** — card is genuine but no numeric grade assigned. Requested via "Auth" on the submission form.
- **Authentic Altered** — card is genuine but shows alteration evidence. Requested via "AA" on the submission form.

See [[no-grade-outcomes]] for the N-codes (N1–N9) that route a card to these labels instead of a number.

### Cross-grade patterns

A few patterns worth noting across the scale `[Certain — derived from above definitions]`:

- **Centering tightens with grade**: 90/10 reverse holds across all grades; front centering relaxes as the grade falls (55/45 at PSA 10 → 90/10 at PSA 3 and below).
- **PSA 1 vs PSA 1.5** differ primarily on whether the card is *intact*. A heavily worn card that's still whole can hit PSA 1.5; missing pieces drop it to PSA 1.
- **PSA 8 vs PSA 9** differ on the *count* of minor flaws: PSA 9 allows exactly one of {slight wax stain, minor printing imperfection, slightly off-white borders}; PSA 8 allows multiple, plus slight corner fraying.

## BGS scale

BGS uses a **10-point scale with half-points at every step** (10, 9.5, 9, 8.5, …, 1.5, 1) `[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]. Unlike PSA, BGS issues **four subgrades** per card (centering, corners, edges, surface) plus a composite overall grade. See [[bgs-subgrades]] for the subgrade mechanics and the Pristine Black/Gold label distinction.

Per-grade subgrade tolerances reproduced below. Half-points (8.5, 7.5, …, 1.5) are not separately defined — BGS describes them explicitly as interpolations: "Cards that are assigned a grade with a half-point increment typically share characteristics from both the level above and the level below" `[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]].

### BGS 10 — Pristine (Black Label)

All four subgrades = 10 `[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 50/50 all around on front. 55/45 or better on back.
- **Corners** — perfect to the naked eye, virtually flawless under intense scrutiny.
- **Edges** — perfect to the naked eye, virtually flawless under intense scrutiny.
- **Surface** — no print spots; flawless color; devoid of registration or focus imperfections; devoid of scratches and metallic print lines.

### BGS 10 — Pristine (Gold Label)

Same criteria as Black Label, but allows **one 9.5 subgrade among three 10s**, in any combination `[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]. The card's overall grade is still 10 Pristine; the Gold-vs-Black label is the only externally-visible difference.

### BGS 9.5 — Gem Mint

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 55/45 both ways on front. 60/40 or better on back.
- **Corners** — virtually flawless to the naked eye; only slight imperfections under magnification.
- **Edges** — virtually flawless to the naked eye; specks of wear visible under magnification.
- **Surface** — virtually flawless to the naked eye; a few extremely minor print imperfections under magnification; deep color, no registration/focus imperfections; devoid of scratches and metallic print lines.

### BGS 9 — Mint

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 55/45 both ways on front. 70/30 or better on back.
- **Corners** — Mint upon close inspection; slight wear allowed under normal scrutiny.
- **Edges** — virtually Mint to the naked eye; unobtrusive specks of chipping on borders allowed.
- **Surface** — a handful of printing specks or one minor spot; very minor focus/color imperfections; clean gloss with one or two tiny scratches barely noticeable to the naked eye; one faint, unobtrusive metallic print line allowed.

### BGS 8 — Near Mint / Mint

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 60/40 both ways or better on front. 80/20 or better on back.
- **Corners** — sharp to the naked eye, slight imperfections allowed under close examination.
- **Edges** — relatively smooth borders; specks of chipping visible to the naked eye allowed.
- **Surface** — a few minor print spots; very minor color/focus imperfections; solid gloss with very minor scratches detectable only on close inspection; or a subtle metallic print line.

### BGS 7 — Near Mint

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 65/35 both ways or better on front. 90/10 or better on back. **Very slight diamond cutting allowed.**
- **Corners** — very minor wear on two or three corners allowed.
- **Edges** — slight roughness, minor chipping, or very minor notching allowed.
- **Surface** — a few noticeable print spots or minor speckling; minor color/focus imperfections; very minor border discoloration; a very minor wax stain on back; solid gloss with a few minor scratches detectable on close inspection; a few metallic print lines.

### BGS 6 — Excellent / Mint

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 70/30 both ways or better on front. 95/5 or better on back. Slight diamond cutting allowed.
- **Corners** — fuzzy corners, but free of dings and fraying.
- **Edges** — moderate roughness, moderate chipping, or minor notching allowed.
- **Surface** — noticeable print spots; minor color/focus imperfections; minor border discoloration; minor wax stains or extremely subtle ink marks; relatively solid gloss with minor scratches, devoid of scuffing; noticeable metallic print lines.

### BGS 5 — Excellent

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 75/25 both ways or better on front. 95/5 or better on back. Slight diamond cutting allowed.
- **Corners** — four fuzzy corners; a touch of notching or a minor ding allowed.
- **Edges** — noticeable roughness — but no layering; very slight notching or noticeable chipping allowed.
- **Surface** — noticeable print spots; minor color/focus imperfections; minor border discoloration; minor wax stains or very light ink mark; some gloss lost from surface with minor scratches, devoid of scuffing.

### BGS 4 — Very Good / Excellent

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 80/20 both ways or better on front. 100/0 or better on back. **Moderate diamond cutting allowed.**
- **Corners** — slight notching or layering, or moderate dings allowed.
- **Edges** — readily chipped or notched and/or slightly layered.
- **Surface** — heavy print spots; hairline creases; moderate color/focus imperfections; moderate border discoloration; moderate wax stains; very light ink mark or tape stain; a good deal of gloss lost; very minor scuffing or an extremely subtle tear.

### BGS 3 — Very Good

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 85/15 both ways or better on front. 100/0 or better on back. Moderate diamond cutting allowed.
- **Corners** — slightly rounded or noticeably notched corners with slight layering allowed.
- **Edges** — heavy notching, moderate layering, or heavy chipping allowed.
- **Surface** — heavy print spots; very minor creases; noticeable color/focus imperfections; noticeable border discoloration; noticeable wax stains; light ink mark or tape stain; very little surface gloss; minor scuffing or a very minor tear.

### BGS 2 — Good

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 90/10 both ways or better on front. 100/0 or offcut on back. **Noticeable diamond cutting allowed.**
- **Corners** — noticeably rounded or heavily notched corners with moderate layering.
- **Edges** — severely chipped, notched, or layered.
- **Surface** — severe print spots; noticeable creases; noticeable color/focus imperfections; noticeable border discoloration; heavy wax stains; moderate ink mark or tape stain; surface devoid of gloss; noticeable scuffing or a noticeable tear.

### BGS 1 — Poor

`[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 100/0 or offcut on front or back. **Heavy diamond cutting allowed.**
- **Corners** — heavily rounded or heavily notched with noticeable layering.
- **Edges** — destructive chipping, notching, or layering.
- **Surface** — severe print spots; heavy creases; severe color/focus imperfections; heavy border discoloration; severe stains; no original gloss; heavy scuffing or a severe tear.

### BGS half-points

8.5, 7.5, 6.5, 5.5, 4.5, 3.5, 2.5, 1.5 — not separately defined. Per BGS: half-point cards "typically share characteristics from both the level above and the level below" `[Certain]` — [[bgs-grading-scale]]. Whether subgrades themselves are issued in half-point increments is not explicit in the captured source `[Likely yes — given the Gold Label rule requires a 9.5 subgrade]`.

## SGC scale

SGC uses a **20-step scale** from 1 (POOR) to 10 PRI (Pristine), with half-grades at every step and a Pristine 10 label above Gem 10 `[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. SGC publishes a **single overall grade** with **no subgrades** and **front-centering only** (no separate back-centering tolerance) — both distinctions from BGS and PSA.

SGC's own copy claims the scale "eliminates the grades known as 'tweeners'." See "Two kinds of SGC half-grade" below for how this claim sits against the published definitions.

### SGC 10 PRI — Pristine

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 50/50.
- **Focus / corners / surface** — virtually flawless; crisp focus, four sharp corners, free of stains, no gloss breaks, no print or refractor lines.
- **Wear** — no visible wear **under magnification**.

### SGC 10 GM — GEM

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 55/45 or better.
- **Focus / corners / surface** — sharp focus, four sharp corners, free of stains, no gloss breaks, no print or refractor lines.
- **Wear** — no visible wear. A slight print spot allowed under close scrutiny if it doesn't detract from aesthetics.

### SGC 9.5 — MINT+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. Relational: "A card that at first glance appears to be Gem Mint 10; upon close inspection it may have a tiny flaw(s) that keeps it from grading GEM MT 10." No independent centering or corner tolerance.

### SGC 9 — MINT

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 60/40 or better.
- **Focus / corners** — sharp focus, four sharp corners.
- **Flaws** — one minor flaw allowed upon close examination: slight corner nick, small gloss break or surface scratch, minor print or refractor line, minor focus or color imperfection, or small print spot.

### SGC 8.5 — NM/MT+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 65/35 or better.
- **Corners** — **four sharp corners** (no wear allowed — distinguishes 8.5 from 8).
- **Flaws** — a few minor flaws upon close examination (same flaw vocabulary as grade 9).

### SGC 8 — NM/MT

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 65/35 or better.
- **Corners** — sharp to the naked eye but **may exhibit slight wear under closer examination**.
- **Flaws** — a few small flaws upon close examination: very minor wear on one corner, gloss break or surface scratch, print or refractor line, focus or color imperfection, print spot.

### SGC 7.5 — NM+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 70/30 or better.
- **Flaws** — a few small flaws upon close examination (grade 8 flaw vocabulary). Corner tolerance unspecified but implicitly tighter than grade 7's "slight wear on some corners."

### SGC 7 — NRMT

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 70/30 or better.
- **Corners** — slight wear on some corners.
- **Flaws** — minor scratching, some print spots or speckling, print or refractor lines acceptable. Card may exhibit a **slightly skewed (diamond) cut**.

### SGC 6.5 — EX/NM+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. Relational: "An EX/NM card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."

### SGC 6 — EX/NM

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 75/25 or better.
- **Corners** — slight fuzzing may be evident.
- **Other** — skewed cut may be more evident, focus or register may be off, slight notching of edges.

### SGC 5.5 — EX+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. Relational: "An EX card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."

### SGC 5 — EX

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 80/20 or better.
- **Corners** — minor rounding or fuzzing.
- **Edges** — roughness or chipping along edge (no layering).
- **Surface** — one VERY slight surface or **"spider" crease** on one side; gloss may be lost with non-detracting scratching.

### SGC 4.5 — VG/EX+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. Relational: "A VG/EX card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."

### SGC 4 — VG/EX

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 85/15 or better.
- **Corners** — slightly rounded with modest surface wear.
- **Surface** — light hairline crease may show on one or both sides; a light tear or surface break may exist.

### SGC 3.5 — VG+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. Relational: "A VG card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."

### SGC 3 — VG

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 90/10 or better.
- **Corners** — more rounded but not excessive.
- **Surface** — stronger creasing may exist; poorer focus, registration, discoloration, and staining more noticeable.

### SGC 2.5 — GOOD+

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]. Relational: "A GOOD card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."

### SGC 2 — GOOD

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 90/10 or better.
- **Defect set** — exhibits one or more of: heavy print spots, heavy crease(s), **pinhole(s)**, color or focus imperfections or discoloration, surface scuffing or tear, rounded and/or fraying corners, ink or pencil marking(s), lack of all or some original gloss.

### SGC 1.5 — FAIR

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 90/10 or better.
- **Defect set** — exhibits **several** of the grade-2 defects; **a small portion of the card may be missing**.

### SGC 1 — POOR

`[Certain]` — [[sgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — not separately specified at this grade.
- **Defect set** — exhibits **many** of the grade-2 defects; **small portions of the card may be missing**.

### Two kinds of SGC half-grade

`[Certain — derived from SGC's own definitions]`. SGC's half-grades fall into two structurally distinct buckets:

- **Criterion-defined:** 9.5 (relational but qualitatively distinct), **8.5** (own centering + four-sharp-corner requirement), **7.5** (own centering). These function as genuine grade levels with content that's not just "more eye appeal."
- **"+" tier:** 6.5, 5.5, 4.5, 3.5, 2.5. Each defined verbatim as "An [X] card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal." These are the top tier within a parent grade bucket — what the broader hobby calls "tweeners." SGC's claim that its scale "eliminates 'tweeners'" doesn't fully hold against this pattern.

## Cross-service grade observations

`[Certain — derived from above]`:

- **Centering tolerances differ at the top.** PSA 10 ≤ 55/45 front; BGS 10 Pristine 50/50 front; SGC 10 PRI 50/50 front, SGC 10 GM 55/45 front. BGS and SGC's *Pristine* tier match each other and are stricter than PSA's *Gem Mint*.
- **Back-centering treatment diverges.** PSA publishes 90/10 back across most of the scale; BGS publishes back tolerances that loosen quickly and dramatically; **SGC publishes no separate back tolerance at all.** Service comparison on back-centering is impossible for SGC.
- **"Diamond cut" terminology is shared by BGS and SGC; PSA doesn't use it.** Surfaces a non-PSA vocabulary cluster worth tracking.
- **"Refractor lines" / "metallic print lines" are named defect categories on BGS and SGC; PSA doesn't name this defect.** Reflects different attention to modern parallels/inserts at the standards level.
- **The "Pristine 10 above Gem 10" pattern is a BGS+SGC convention.** PSA does not have a Pristine label; its top is Gem Mint 10. The mechanism differs between BGS (subgrade composition) and SGC (stricter top-level criteria).
- **Half-grade philosophy varies sharply.** PSA: only 1.5 and 8.5 exist, fully criterion-defined. BGS: half-points at every step, explicitly described as interpolations. SGC: half-grades at every step, mixed — some criterion-defined, most positioned as "+" within a parent grade.

Cross-service equivalence claims (e.g., "BGS 9.5 ≈ PSA 10 ≈ SGC 10 GM") are common in collector discourse but are **not supported** by either the published standards or the differing centering/defect vocabularies `[Likely]`. Any synthesis page making such a claim should flag it as market consensus, not standard.

## CGC scale

CGC uses a **20-grade scale** (1 → Pristine 10) with half-grades at every step `[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]. CGC publishes a **single overall grade** with **no subgrades**. Uniquely among the four major graders covered here, CGC **explicitly distinguishes TCG criteria from sports/non-sports criteria** at several grade levels. See [[entities/cgc]] for label history (the "Gem Mint 9.5 → Gem Mint 10" relabeling).

### CGC 10 — Pristine

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 50/50.
- Virtually flawless to the naked eye; perfect color and registration.
- Special Pristine 10 label issued on the holder.

### CGC 10 — Gem Mint

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — 55/45 front, 75/25 back.
- Overall 10 grade; one criterion does not meet Pristine 10 requirements.
- Corners appear perfect to naked eye, Mint+ under 10× magnification.
- Surface free of print spots, perfect gloss, no surface flaws.

### CGC 9.5 — Mint+

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]. Qualitative: "displays premium eye appeal for a Mint card. Qualities such as exceptional centering, surface qualities/color or other key elements can elevate a card to a Mint+ grade." No published centering floor.

### CGC 9 — Mint

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering (sports/non-sports)** — 60/40 front, 90/10 back.
- Four sharp corners; slight minor flaws on edges may be visible.
- Surface: original gloss intact; small number of specks or one minor spot or surface defect allowed.
- TCG: only a few minor manufacturing or handling defects.

### CGC 8.5 — NM/Mint+

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering** — not stated for sports/non-sports (gap vs. grade 8).
- Relatively smooth edges, minor touches of wear; original color borders and gloss.
- One of the following very minor flaws allowed: corners sharp to naked eye but slight imperfections under magnification; small amount of minor print spots; subtle focus imperfections.
- Very slight diamond cut allowed.
- TCG: small handling defects allowed.

### CGC 8 — NM/Mint

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering (sports/non-sports)** — 65/35.
- Same flaw allowances as 8.5; very slight diamond cut allowed.
- TCG: small-to-moderate handling defects (looser than 8.5's "small").

### CGC 7.5 — Near Mint+

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering (sports/non-sports)** — 65/35.
- Touch of wear on two or three corners or slightly rough edges.
- Image may be slightly out of register; slight diamond cut allowed.
- Very slight wax staining allowed on reverse.
- TCG: moderate defect or a number of small handling defects.

### CGC 7 — Near Mint

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering (sports/non-sports)** — 70/30.
- Touch of wear on three or more corners and/or slightly rough edges.
- Image may be slightly out of register; slight diamond cut allowed.
- Very slight wax staining on reverse.

### CGC 6.5 — Ex/NM+

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- No more than one slightly "dinged" corner, or no more than two of: two or three fuzzy corners; slightly rough edges; noticeable print spots.
- Moderate diamond cut allowed; light wax staining on front acceptable.

### CGC 6 — Ex/NM

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering (sports/non-sports)** — 75/25.
- No more than one "dinged" corner, or no more than two of: two or three fuzzy corners; slightly rough edges; noticeable print spots.
- Moderate diamond cut; wax staining on front acceptable.

### CGC 5.5 — Excellent+

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- Corners may exhibit light fuzziness and very minor rounding; may come to a point but one or two "dinged" corners allowed.
- Chipping on edges; minor border discoloration; noticeable print spots; color or focus imperfections.

### CGC 5 — Excellent

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- Corners may exhibit fuzziness and very minor rounding; two to three "dinged" corners allowed.
- Chipping on edges; minor border discoloration; noticeable print spots; color or focus imperfections.
- On scratch-off surface cards, coating may be removed but surface not damaged.

### CGC 4.5 — VG/Ex+

`[Certain]` — [[cgc-grading-scale]]:

- **Centering (sports/non-sports)** — 85/15; extremely noticeable diamond cut allowed.

## Cross-service grade observations

`[Certain — derived from above]`:

- **Centering tolerances differ at the top.** PSA 10 ≤ 55/45 front; BGS 10 Pristine 50/50 front; SGC 10 PRI 50/50 front, SGC 10 GM 55/45 front; CGC Pristine 10 50/50, CGC Gem Mint 10 55/45 front / 75/25 back. BGS, SGC, and CGC's *Pristine* tier all converge on 50/50; PSA's single top grade is 55/45.
- **Back-centering treatment diverges.** PSA publishes 90/10 back across most of the scale; BGS publishes back tolerances that loosen quickly and dramatically; SGC publishes **no separate back tolerance at all**; CGC publishes 75/25 at Gem Mint 10 and 90/10 at Mint 9, with gaps in between.
- **"Diamond cut" terminology is shared by BGS, SGC, and CGC; PSA doesn't use it.**
- **"Refractor lines" / "metallic print lines" are named defect categories on BGS and SGC; CGC and PSA don't name this defect** in their published standards.
- **The "Pristine 10 above Gem Mint 10" pattern is shared by BGS, SGC, and CGC.** PSA has no Pristine label; its top is Gem Mint 10. The mechanism differs: BGS uses subgrade composition; SGC and CGC use stricter top-level criteria.
- **Half-grade philosophy varies.** PSA: only 1.5 and 8.5, criterion-defined. BGS: half-points at every step, explicitly interpolations. SGC: half-grades at every step, mixed (some criterion-defined, most relational "+"). CGC: half-grades at every step, all criterion-defined or qualitatively distinct.
- **TCG card criteria are uniquely published by CGC.** PSA and BGS do not publish separate criteria for trading card games vs. sports cards in their publicly accessible grading standards. CGC distinguishes them explicitly from grade 9 down.
- **Cross-service equivalence claims** (e.g., "BGS 9.5 ≈ PSA 10") are common in collector discourse but are **not supported** by the published standards or differing centering/defect vocabularies `[Likely]`. Any synthesis page making such a claim should flag it as market consensus, not standard.

# No-grade outcomes

## TL;DR

A card submitted to PSA can come back without a numeric grade. PSA documents 9 distinct N-codes (N1–N9) for "why this card got no number," each with specific fee and re-encapsulation policies. Cards that pass authentication but flunk numeric grading may still be encapsulated as "Authentic" or "Authentic Altered" if the submitter pre-requested it `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

## Encapsulation labels (non-numeric)

`[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- **Authentic** — genuine card, no numeric grade. Requested via "Auth" on the submission form as a minimum grade.
- **Authentic Altered** — genuine card with alteration evidence. Requested via "AA" on the submission form as a minimum grade.

These labels are not failures — they're the submitter's pre-authorized fallback when a numeric grade isn't reachable.

## The 9 N-codes

Each code is what PSA reports back when no numeric grade is assigned `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]].

### N1 — Evidence of Trimming

Card edge appears altered (scissors, scalpel, cutter, or other sharp instrument). Telltale signs: hooked edge appearance, unusually sharp edges for the issue, inconsistent tone along the edge, wavy or unnatural edges.

- **Fee**: charged.
- **AA-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "AA" was on the submission form.

### N2 — Evidence of Restoration

Paper stock has been built up (e.g., ripped corners rebuilt to look new).

- **Fee**: charged.
- **AA-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "AA" was on the submission form.

### N3 — Evidence of Recoloration

Color artificially improved.

- **Fee**: charged.
- **AA-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "AA" was on the submission form.

### N4 — Questionable Authenticity

Card appears counterfeit, or autograph deemed not genuine.

- **Fee**: charged.
- **AA-eligible**: **no**. Never qualifies for any encapsulation under any circumstance.

### N5 — Altered Stock

Paper restoration, crease/wrinkle pressing, scratch removal, enhanced gloss, or foreign substances applied to the surface (cleaning spray, wax).

- **Fee**: charged.
- **AA-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "AA" was on the submission form.

### N6 — Minimum Size Requirement

Card is significantly undersized vs factory specifications.

- **Fee**: **not charged** by default.
- **Auth-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "Auth" was on the submission form (fee charged in this case).

### N7 — Evidence of Cleaning

Whitener used on borders; or solution to remove wax / candy / gum / tobacco stains; or any foreign substance applied to the surface.

- **Fee**: charged.
- **AA-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "AA" was on the submission form.

### N8 — Miscut

Factory cut is abnormal for the issue, causing edges to deviate from intended appearance.

- **Fee**: **not charged** by default.
- **Auth-eligible**: yes, at grader's discretion, if "Auth" was on the submission form (fee charged in this case).

### N9 — Don't Grade

PSA does not grade or cannot encapsulate the issue (oversized cards, obscure issues).

- **Fee**: **not charged**.
- **Auth-eligible**: no.
- **Reversal possible**: if the submitter provides additional verified or hobby-approved resources for the issue via a "Research Request" through PSA's Customer Request Center (CRC), the decision could be reversed on resubmittal.

## Submission-form options that matter

`[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- **"AA"** as minimum grade → card eligible for "Authentic Altered" encapsulation when an N-code is the alternative.
- **"Auth"** as minimum grade → card eligible for "Authentic" encapsulation in the N6 / N8 cases.
- **MG (Minimum Grade)** option → only available on select service tiers; lets submitter set the lowest acceptable numeric grade.

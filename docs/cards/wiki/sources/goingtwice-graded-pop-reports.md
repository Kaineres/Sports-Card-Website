# Graded Pop Reports — What Do They Mean? (Going Twice)

## For future Claude
This page summarizes a Going Twice blog article (goingtwice.com/blogs/grading) — covers all three major grading companies' (PSA, BGS, CGC) free public pop reports with direct URLs, grade distribution as a signal of manufacturing print quality, and a cross-grader hit-rate comparison for the same card.
It feeds: [[concepts/population-report]], [[entities/psa]], [[entities/bgs]], [[entities/cgc]].
Key fact: the Umbreon VMAX Alt Art cross-grader comparison shows PSA awarded a 10 to 80% of submissions vs. CGC's 13% Pristine rate — higher PSA 10 hit rates create more supply on the market, which may suppress individual PSA 10 prices relative to rarer CGC Pristines.

## TL;DR

- All three major grading companies (PSA, BGS, CGC) publish free, publicly accessible pop reports; the same card shows different grade distributions across companies.
- Grade distribution reveals manufacturing print quality — low-grade clustering signals production defects in that issue.
- Grading company choice affects both hit rate and market supply: same card = 80% PSA 10 vs. 13% CGC Pristine — more PSA 10s on the market may suppress individual PSA 10 value.

## Key claims

- Pop reports are maintained by PSA, BGS, and CGC. Public URLs `[Certain]`:
  - PSA: psacard.com/pop
  - CGC: cgccards.com/population-report
  - BGS: beckett.com/grading/pop-report
- Best search method for accuracy: card number (not name, which can have variant spellings). `[Certain]`
- Grade distribution reveals print quality. Cards with poor print quality (e.g., Burning Shadows Charizard) show lower-grade clustering across the population. `[Certain]`
- For vintage cards, low high-grade populations are expected — pack-fresh condition after decades is rare. `[Certain]`
- High submission volume signals collector demand but can be "at odds with the rarity of the card." `[Certain]` — direct quote.
- Cross-grader comparison for Umbreon VMAX Alt Art (Pokémon Evolving Skies): PSA 80% received 10; CGC 13% received Pristine/Perfect 10; BGS 31% received Pristine 10 or Black Label. PSA offers a higher chance of hitting a 10, but creates more PSA 10s on market — may suppress individual PSA 10 value. `[Certain]`
- Choosing where to grade should factor in: (a) probability of hitting target grade; (b) supply of that grade already on the market. `[Certain]`

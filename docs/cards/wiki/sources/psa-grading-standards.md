## Key claims

### Top-level ungradable rule

`[Certain]`. From `PSA Grading Standards 12.md`: "PSA will not grade cards that bear evidence of trimming, re-coloring, restoration, or any other forms of tampering, or are of questionable authenticity." This is the unifying summary that the N1–N5 / N7 codes formalize.

### Numeric grades (1–10)

`[Certain]`. Full definitions are reproduced in [[grading-scales]]. Headline distinctions:

- **PSA 10 (Gem Mint)** — virtually perfect; centering ≤ ~55/45 front, ≤ ~75/25 reverse.
- **PSA 9 (Mint)** — exactly one minor flaw allowed; centering ~60/40 front, ~90/10 reverse.
- **PSA 8 (NM-MT)** — multiple minor flaws permitted; centering ~65/35 front, ~90/10 reverse.
- **PSA 7 (NM)** — slight surface wear visible only on close inspection; ~70/30 front, ~90/10 back.
- **PSA 6 (EX-MT)** through **PSA 4 (VG-EX)** — progressively more visible wear; front centering relaxes 80/20 → 85/15.
- **PSA 3 (VG)** and below — 90/10 centering on both sides; condition issues become substantial (rounding, scuffing, creases, discoloration).
- **PSA 1.5 (Fair)** — card must be *fully intact*; missing pieces drop it to PSA 1.
- **PSA 1 (Poor)** — defects so severe that eye appeal has nearly vanished.
- **PSA 8.5** — text definition not yet ingested.

### Encapsulation labels

`[Certain]`:

- **Authentic** and **Authentic Altered** are non-numeric labels. "AA" / "Auth" on submission form determines eligibility.
- Half-grades 1.5 and 8.5 exist for cards. **Pins and coins do not get half-grades**.

### Qualifier — MK (Marks)

`[Certain]`. Any card with writing, ink marks, pencil marks, or evidence of impression left from the act of writing is designated "MK."

### Other qualifier categories (codes not captured)

`[Certain that the categories exist; codes and definitions not in this source]`. Referenced via image filenames: miscut, off-center, staining, print defect, out-of-focus.

### 9 "No Grade" codes (N1–N9)

`[Certain]`. Fully reproduced in [[no-grade-outcomes]]:

- N1 Trimming, N2 Restoration, N3 Recoloration, N5 Altered Stock, N7 Cleaning — fee charged; AA-eligible if requested.
- N4 Questionable Authenticity — fee charged; never eligible for any encapsulation.
- N6 Minimum Size, N8 Miscut — no fee by default; Auth-eligible if requested (fee charged then).
- N9 Don't Grade — no fee; reversal possible via Research Request through CRC.

### Policy carve-outs

`[Certain]`:

- **Hand-cut cards** (Bazooka, Post Cereal, Hostess, strip cards): graded if visible/defined borders are retained.
- **Sheet-cut cards from non-sheet issues**: not graded (example: 1979 OPC Wayne Gretzky).
- **Pins / coins**: separate framework focused on eye appeal; no half-grades.

### Subjectivity

`[Certain]`. Most grading is objective (print defects, staining, surface wrinkles, centering), but a subjective component exists for centering and eye appeal. Graders may raise or lower a borderline grade based on eye-appeal strength and "what the market will accept" for the specific issue. Examples in source: 1955 vs 1957 Topps Sandy Koufax — same off-centering issue produces opposite grader judgments depending on how visible the centering is on each issue's design.

### MG (Minimum Grade) option

`[Certain]`. Only available on select service tiers.

# Official Trading Card Grading Service

## TL;DR

- PSA's public-facing services page. Describes their authentication + grading process, 14 service tiers (pricing × turnaround × insured-value cap), and adjacent products (Set Registry, Price Guide, CardFacts, Auction Prices).
- Primary source — PSA describing PSA. Authoritative for PSA's own offerings; **not** neutral for competitive comparisons.
- Pricing and turnaround data is volatile; treat captured numbers as `[Certain as of 2026-05-18]`.

## Key claims

- PSA describes itself as "the largest and most respected third-party authentication and grading company in the world for trading cards and memorabilia" `[Certain in source]`. Self-described — independent verification would be needed to treat this as objective fact rather than marketing.
- Grading uses a 10-point scale; 10 is best `[Certain in source]`. Detailed grade definitions are not in this source.
- The process is two-step: (1) **authentication** — verify genuineness, detect doctoring such as re-coloring or trimming; (2) **grading** — condition assessment on the 1–10 scale. Authentication must pass before grading occurs `[Certain in source]`.
- After grading, every card is encapsulated in a "sonically sealed, tamper-evident" hard plastic case. The PSA LightHouse™ label displays the grade and a unique certification number `[Certain in source]`.
- Service tiers (as of 2026-05-18) range from **$24.99/card** ("Value Bulk", Collectors Club members only, $500 insured cap, 140–160 business day turnaround, 50-card minimum per era) to **$9,999+/card** ("Premium 10", $250,001+ insured cap, 5–7 business day turnaround) `[Certain in source]`. 14 tiers total.
- Higher-priced tiers add features absent from lower ones: high-resolution Premium Imaging, fitted sleeve protector inside the slab, Grader Notes documenting defects `[Certain in source]`.
- PSA operates adjacent products mentioned but not detailed here: **PSA Set Registry**, **PSA Price Guide**, **PSA CardFacts**, **PSA Auction Prices**, **PSA Security** verification `[Certain in source]`. Each warrants its own page after further ingest.

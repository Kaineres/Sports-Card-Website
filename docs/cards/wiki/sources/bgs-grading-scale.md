# BGS Grading Scale

## For future Claude
This page summarizes BGS's official grading scale (beckett.com/grading), captured across 12 raw files named "Back Button.md" through "Back Button 11.md" due to Web Clipper mislabeling — covers integer grades 1–10 plus the 9.5 Gem Mint level and the four-subgrade (centering/corners/edges/surface) composite model.
It feeds: [[entities/bgs]], [[concepts/bgs-subgrades]], [[concepts/grading-scales]], [[concepts/card-grading]].
Key fact: half-grades 8.5, 7.5, etc. have no separately published criteria — BGS explicitly defines them only as interpolations sharing characteristics of the grades above and below, which is the complete published standard, not a capture gap.

## TL;DR

- Beckett Grading Services' official 1–10 grading scale, captured across **12 raw files** — one per grade-tab click on `beckett.com/grading`. File-naming reflects the Web Clipper's misidentification of a "Back Button" element as the page title, not the grade contained. Mapping table below.
- BGS uses a **four-subgrade model** (centering, corners, edges, surface) plus a composite overall grade — structurally different from PSA's single-number grade. See [[bgs-subgrades]] for the mechanics.
- **Half-points (8.5, 7.5, …, 1.5) are not separately defined.** `[Certain]` BGS publishes criteria only for integer grades plus 9.5. Half-grades are explicitly described as interpolations: cards "typically share characteristics from both the level above and the level below." This is the complete published standard, not a missing-clip gap.
- The **10 Pristine grade has two label variants** — Black Label (perfect 10 across all four subgrades) and Gold Label (one 9.5 subgrade allowed among three 10s). `[Certain]` BGS-unique distinction with strong collector/market relevance.
- BGS-specific terminology: **"diamond cutting"** (centering defect, appears in grades 7 and below); systematically looser **back-centering** tolerances vs front (e.g., 9.5 = 55/45 front but 60/40 back). `[Certain]`
- No pricing / service-tier content captured in this clip set — scale only.

## Key claims

### Numeric grades (integer + 9.5)

`[Certain]`. Full subgrade tolerances reproduced in [[grading-scales]] under the BGS section. Headline distinctions:

- **10 Pristine — Black Label** — Centering 50/50 front, 55/45 or better back. Corners/edges perfect to the naked eye and virtually flawless under intense scrutiny. Surface devoid of scratches, metallic print lines, registration imperfections.
- **10 Pristine — Gold Label** — Same criteria as Black Label, **but one 9.5 subgrade is allowed** among the four (one 9.5 + three 10s, in any combination).
- **9.5 Gem Mint** — 55/45 front, 60/40+ back. Virtually flawless to naked eye; only slight imperfections allowed under magnification.
- **9 Mint** — 55/45 front, 70/30+ back. Slight corner wear allowed under normal scrutiny.
- **8 NM-MT** — 60/40 front, 80/20+ back. Sharp corners to the naked eye; slight imperfections under close examination.
- **7 NM** — 65/35 front, 90/10+ back. **"Very slight diamond cutting allowed."** Minor wear on 2–3 corners.
- **6 EX-MT** — 70/30 front, 95/5+ back. Fuzzy corners free of dings/fraying. "Slight diamond cutting allowed."
- **5 EX** — 75/25 front, 95/5+ back. Four fuzzy corners; touch of notching or minor ding allowed.
- **4 VG-EX** — 80/20 front, 100/0+ back. "Moderate diamond cutting allowed." Slight notching/layering allowed.
- **3 VG** — 85/15 front, 100/0+ back. Slightly rounded or noticeably notched corners with slight layering.
- **2 GD** — 90/10 front, 100/0 or offcut back. "Noticeable diamond cutting allowed." Heavily notched corners with moderate layering.
- **1 PR** — 100/0 or offcut on either side. "Heavy diamond cutting allowed." Heavily rounded corners with noticeable layering; destructive edge damage; severe stains; no original gloss.

### Half-point grades

`[Certain]`. From every grade page verbatim: "Beckett Grading Services provides final grades in half-point increments (i.e., 10, 9.5, 9, 8.5 etc.). Cards that are assigned a grade with a half-point increment typically share characteristics from both the level above and the level below the actual grade given." No separate criteria are published for 8.5, 7.5, 6.5, 5.5, 4.5, 3.5, 2.5, or 1.5.

### Four-subgrade composite

`[Certain]`. Every grade definition lists tolerances for four subgrade dimensions: **centering**, **corners**, **edges**, **surface**. The overall grade is composited from these four subgrades. See [[bgs-subgrades]] for full mechanics.

### Pristine label variants

`[Certain]`. From `Back Button 1.md`: "The only difference between a black label 10 pristine and a gold label 10 pristine is the gold label 10 pristine allows for one small imperfection under magnification resulting in ONE 9.5 and THREE 10's in any combination of subgrade categories."

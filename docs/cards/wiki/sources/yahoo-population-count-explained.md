# Sports Card Investing: Population Count Explained (Yahoo Sports / The Hockey News)

## For future Claude
This page summarizes a Yahoo Sports / The Hockey News article (sports.yahoo.com) explaining PSA pop counts — covers the definitions of "Population" and "Population Higher," the pricing dynamics of high-POP vs. low-POP cards, and a concrete modern-card growth example using the 2023-24 Connor Bedard Young Guns.
It feeds: [[concepts/population-report]].
Key fact: only the first ~80 lines of the raw file are card-relevant — the remainder is a Knicks-Cavs Eastern Conference Finals preview that was excluded; do not re-ingest the full file without filtering.

## TL;DR

- "POP count" = number of graded cards with the same grade; "Population Higher" = count of cards graded above that level. PSA does not issue half-grades at grade 9 or above.
- High-POP cards are liquid and easy to price; low-POP cards give the seller pricing power with fewer comps.
- Modern in-demand cards can grow population rapidly — Connor Bedard Young Guns added ~75 new PSA 9/10 copies per day in its first 80 days.

## Key claims

- "Population" = number of cards with a given grade entered into the PSA system. `[Certain]`
- "Population Higher" = number of cards graded above the searched grade. `[Certain]`
- PSA does not issue half-grades at PSA 9 and above. `[Certain]` — stated directly; consistent with [[concepts/grading-scales]] which documents only 1.5 and 8.5 as half-grades.
- POP count is "your competition" as a buyer or seller — it reflects how many other sellers have the same card at the same grade. `[Certain]`
- High-POP cards: many comparable sales make pricing straightforward; seller competition may push prices to 80–90% of recent comps. `[Certain]`
- Low-POP cards: fewer comps, fewer competing sellers; seller can set the price. "Yesterday's price is not today's price." `[Certain]`
- Connor Bedard Young Guns (2023-24 Upper Deck): 6,000+ PSA 9/10 graded in 80 days after release (~75 per day). Illustrates rapid population growth for modern in-demand cards. `[Certain — point-in-time snapshot]`
- Bedard Outburst parallel (1:3,000 hobby pack odds vs. 1:100 base) had 5,959 fewer PSA 9/10 copies than base — print rarity directly drives population differential. `[Certain — point-in-time snapshot]`

# How to Use PSA and BGS Pop Reports to Your Advantage (Sundo Cards)

## For future Claude
This page summarizes a Sundo Cards article (sundocards.com) — covers the four-step pop report generation pipeline (submission → evaluation → logging → public access), cross-grading inflation (same physical card counted in both PSA and BGS totals), and within-grade quality variation that pop counts do not capture.
It feeds: [[concepts/population-report]], [[entities/psa]], [[entities/bgs]].
Key fact: the raw file captured two separate articles in one page; only the pop report article (~first half) is relevant here — the embedded "Print Lines on Trading Cards" article that follows is a distinct topic and was excluded from ingest.

## TL;DR

- Pop report data is generated by a four-step pipeline: Submission → Evaluation → Logging → Public Access.
- Re-grading and cross-grading both inflate aggregate counts; the same physical card can appear in PSA and BGS totals simultaneously.
- Pop reports don't capture within-grade quality variation — not all PSA 10s are identical.

## Key claims

- Pop report generation pipeline: (1) collector submits card; (2) graders evaluate and assign grade; (3) grade logged into database; (4) data made publicly available via pop report tool. `[Certain]`
- Reports are continuously updated as more cards are submitted. `[Certain]`
- Cards not submitted, kept in private collections, damaged, or lost are invisible to pop reports — actual population could be higher or lower. `[Certain]`
- Cross-grading inflates counts: a PSA 9 submitted to BGS and upgraded to 9.5 appears in both PSA and BGS population totals as separate entries, even though only one physical card exists. `[Certain]`
- Within-grade quality variation: two PSA 10s can have different centering or surface quality; the pop report does not capture this distinction and the market may value them differently. `[Certain]`
- 1999 Pokémon Base Set Charizard example: ~120 PSA 10s (at time of writing) cited as justifying premium pricing given iconic status and low high-grade population. `[Certain as example — not current data]`

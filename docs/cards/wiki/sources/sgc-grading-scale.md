# SGC Grading Scale

## For future Claude
This page summarizes SGC's official grading scale (gosgc.com/card-grading/scale), captured across 21 raw files — one intro plus one per grade tab — covering all 20 steps from Poor 1 to Pristine 10 PRI; this is the cleanest multi-file capture of all four grading-service ingests with no missing tabs.
It feeds: [[entities/sgc]], [[concepts/grading-scales]], [[concepts/card-grading]].
Key fact: SGC's own intro copy claims the scale "eliminates the grades known as tweeners," but five of the nine half-grades (6.5, 5.5, 4.5, 3.5, 2.5) are textbook tweeners defined only as "high-end overall quality and eye appeal" — the self-claim is internally inconsistent.

## TL;DR

- SGC's official grading scale page, captured across **21 raw files** — one intro file plus one per grade tab. Every grade tab clipped successfully; this is the cleanest capture of the three grading-service ingests so far.
- SGC publishes a **20-step scale** spanning 1 → 10 PRI, with half-grades at every step and a Pristine 10 distinction above Gem 10.
- **No subgrades** — unlike BGS, SGC issues a single overall grade. **No back-centering tolerances published** — unlike both PSA and BGS, SGC publishes only one centering figure per grade (front-implied).
- Vocabulary parallels BGS more than PSA: SGC uses **"diamond cut"** (matches BGS's "diamond cutting") and names **"refractor lines"** as a defect category (parallels BGS's "metallic print lines"). PSA's published standards use neither term.
- Half-grade philosophy is internally inconsistent: SGC's own copy claims the scale "eliminates the grades known as tweeners," yet most half-grades (9.5, 6.5, 5.5, 4.5, 3.5, 2.5) are positioned only as the "high-end overall quality and eye appeal" tier of their parent grade — textbook tweeners. Two half-grades (**8.5 NM/MT+** and **7.5 NM+**) have genuinely distinct published criteria. See [[sgc]] and [[grading-scales]] for full breakdown.

## Key claims

### Tweener-elimination self-claim

`[Certain in source, Likely-Internally-Inconsistent]`. From the intro page: "SGC uses a scale which eliminates the grades known as 'tweeners': no grading scale is more accurate or consistent." This claim is partially undermined by SGC's own grade definitions — see "Two kinds of half-grade" below.

### Numeric grades

`[Certain]`. Full criteria reproduced in [[grading-scales]] under the SGC section. Headline distinctions:

- **10 PRI (Pristine)** — "virtually flawless"; 50/50 centering, crisp focus, four sharp corners, free of stains, no gloss breaks, no print or refractor lines, no visible wear **under magnification**.
- **10 GM (GEM)** — 55/45 or better centering, sharp focus, four sharp corners, free of stains, no gloss breaks, no print or refractor lines, no visible wear. A slight print spot under close scrutiny allowed if it doesn't detract from aesthetics.
- **9.5 (MINT+)** — relational: "appears to be Gem Mint 10 at first glance" with "tiny flaw(s) that keep it from grading GEM MT 10." No independent centering or corner tolerance.
- **9 (MINT)** — 60/40 or better centering, sharp focus, four sharp corners. One minor flaw allowed (slight corner nick, small gloss break / surface scratch, minor print or refractor line, minor focus/color imperfection, small print spot).
- **8.5 (NM/MT+)** — 65/35 or better centering, **four sharp corners** (no wear allowed). A few minor flaws of the type listed at grade 9.
- **8 (NM/MT)** — 65/35 or better centering, corners sharp to the naked eye but **may exhibit slight wear under closer examination**. A few small flaws allowed.
- **7.5 (NM+)** — 70/30 or better centering, a few small flaws may exist under close examination (same flaw vocabulary as grade 8). Corner tolerance unspecified — implicitly tighter than grade 7's "slight wear on some corners."
- **7 (NRMT)** — 70/30 or better centering, slight wear on some corners, minor scratching, some print spots/speckling, print or refractor lines acceptable, card may exhibit a **slightly skewed (diamond) cut**.
- **6.5 (EX/NM+)** — relational: "An EX/NM card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."
- **6 (EX/NM)** — 75/25 or better centering, slight fuzzing of corners may be evident, skewed cut may be more evident, focus or register may be off, slight notching of edges.
- **5.5 (EX+)** — relational: "An EX card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."
- **5 (EX)** — 80/20 or better centering, minor rounding or fuzzing of corners, roughness/chipping along edge (no layering), one **VERY slight surface or "spider" crease** on one side, gloss may be lost with non-detracting scratching.
- **4.5 (VG/EX+)** — relational: "A VG/EX card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."
- **4 (VG/EX)** — 85/15 or better centering, slightly rounded corners with modest surface wear; **light hairline crease on one or both sides**; a light tear or surface break may exist.
- **3.5 (VG+)** — relational: "A VG card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."
- **3 (VG)** — 90/10 or better centering, more-rounded-but-not-excessive corners, **stronger creasing may exist**; poorer focus, registration, discoloration, and staining more noticeable.
- **2.5 (GOOD+)** — relational: "A GOOD card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal."
- **2 (GOOD)** — 90/10 or better centering, exhibits one or more of: heavy print spots, heavy crease(s), **pinhole(s)**, color/focus imperfections or discoloration, surface scuffing or tear, rounded/fraying corners, ink or pencil marking(s), lack of original gloss.
- **1.5 (FAIR)** — 90/10 or better centering, exhibits **several** of the grade-2 defects, **a small portion of the card may be missing**.
- **1 (POOR)** — exhibits **many** of the grade-2 defects, **small portions of the card may be missing** (centering threshold not stated for grade 1 specifically).

### Two kinds of half-grade

`[Certain — derived from above]`:

- **Criterion-defined half-grades:** 9.5 (relational but qualitatively distinct), **8.5** (own centering and corner tolerances), **7.5** (own centering tolerance). These act as genuine grade levels.
- **"+" tier half-grades:** 6.5, 5.5, 4.5, 3.5, 2.5 all defined verbatim as "An [X] card that exhibits high-end overall quality and eye appeal." These act as the *top tier within a parent grade bucket* rather than independent levels.

### Centering tolerances published front-only

`[Certain — by omission]`. SGC publishes one centering figure per grade. PSA and BGS both publish front + back; SGC does not. This makes cross-service centering comparisons for SGC asymmetric.

### Defect terminology

`[Certain]`. SGC's defect vocabulary partially overlaps BGS but not PSA:

- **"Diamond cut"** — used at grade 7; matches BGS's "diamond cutting." PSA's published standards do not use this term.
- **"Refractor lines"** — named defect category at grades 7–9 (parallel to "print lines"). Parallels BGS's "metallic print lines." PSA does not name this defect.
- **"Spider crease"** — used at grade 5. Term not used in PSA or BGS standards.
- **"Pinhole"** — named defect at grades 1, 1.5, 2. Term not used in PSA or BGS standards.

# PSA

## For future Claude
PSA (Professional Sports Authenticator) is the dominant third-party card grading service by volume, covering authentication, numeric grading, and encapsulation of trading cards.
Relevant when: answering questions about PSA grade definitions, authentication N-codes, service tiers, the PSA Population Report, hand-cut card policies, or comparing PSA to BGS/SGC/CGC.
Key fact: PSA uses integer grades 1–10 with half-grades only at 1.5 and 8.5; authentication screening (9 N-codes) must pass before grading; every graded card is encapsulated with a unique LightHouse™ certification number; the PSA pop report is the most widely cited in the hobby and is updated daily.

## TL;DR

PSA is a third-party trading card authentication and grading company. They authenticate cards for genuineness, grade condition on a 1–10 scale (with half-grades at 1.5 and 8.5), and encapsulate graded cards in tamper-evident slabs `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-service]], [[psa-grading-standards]].

## What PSA does

- **Authentication** — verifies a card is genuine; screens for trimming, restoration, recoloration, altered stock, cleaning, miscut, and questionable authenticity. Full screen taxonomy is the 9 N-codes detailed in [[no-grade-outcomes]] `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-service]], [[psa-grading-standards]].
- **Grading** — assesses condition on a 10-point scale, 10 being best. Half-grades at 1.5 and 8.5. Grading only happens if authentication passes. See [[grading-scales]] and [[grading-qualifiers]] `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-service]], [[psa-grading-standards]].
- **Encapsulation** — every graded card is sealed in a sonically-sealed, tamper-evident plastic case ("slab"). The PSA LightHouse™ label displays the grade and a unique certification number `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-service]].

## Ungradable cards

PSA states explicitly: cards bearing evidence of **trimming, re-coloring, restoration, any other forms of tampering, or questionable authenticity** will not be graded `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]. This is the top-level rejection rule that the N1–N5 / N7 codes operationalize in [[no-grade-outcomes]].

## Policy carve-outs

`[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]:

- **Hand-cut cards** (Bazooka, Post Cereal, Hostess, strip cards, etc.) — graded, provided the card retains its visible/defined borders. Cards cut beyond the border get "Authentic" encapsulation only. Severely undersized may be rejected.
- **Sheet-cut cards from non-sheet issues** — **not graded**. Example: a 1979 O-Pee-Chee Wayne Gretzky cut from a sheet won't be graded, because that card was originally issued in pack form, not as a sheet.
- **Pins / coins** — graded under a different framework focused on eye appeal (scratches, dents, rust, centering, compression, paper condition, print quality). **No half-grades** for pins/coins.

## Grader subjectivity

PSA officially acknowledges that grading at the boundary between grades involves judgment calls based on **eye appeal** and **market acceptability** for the specific issue `[Certain]` — [[psa-grading-standards]]. See [[card-grading]] for examples.

## Population Report

PSA maintains the most widely cited pop report in the hobby — a daily-updated, publicly searchable database of every card PSA has graded, broken down by set, player, and grade `[Certain]` — [[sources/psa-market-report-population-perspective]], [[sources/packz-psa-population-report-guide]].

Accessible at psacard.com/pop (by player or set) or psacard.com/cert (by individual certification number). Each card entry shows a **Population** count at each grade and a **Population Higher** count above each grade.

Key caveats per PSA's own article (Joe Orlando) `[Certain]` — [[sources/psa-market-report-population-perspective]]:
- Numbers represent only PSA-graded cards, not all cards in existence.
- Excluded: raw/unsubmitted cards, cards at other graders, cards rejected for alteration.
- Low pop can mean nobody submitted, not genuine scarcity — see [[concepts/population-report]] for the "$500 for a $10 card" example.

For full discussion of pop report mechanics, limitations, and value implications, see [[concepts/population-report]].

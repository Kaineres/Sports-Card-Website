Population count, commonly referred to as "POP count" is a term that accounts for the number of graded cards with the same score. For today's article, we will reference  
Professional Sports Authenticator (PSA) scores.

2023-24 Upper Deck Connor Bedard Young Guns PSA 9

The Connor Bedard Young Guns rookie card from 2023-24 Upper Deck Series Two is one of the hottest cards in the entire sports card industry. Collectors and breakers are pulling hundreds of Bedard Young Guns on a daily basis, with many being submitted to PSA for grading.

![PSA population report for Connor Bedard Young Guns PSA 9 and PSA 10 grades](https://s.yimg.com/ny/api/res/1.2/eJ6tM4zykAgrGDinPGI7Lg--/YXBwaWQ9aGlnaGxhbmRlcjt3PTk2MDtoPTYxOTtjZj13ZWJw/https://media.zenfs.com/en/the_hockey_news_articles_331/ea7b22afbf351d09be3337fdecadf70e)

PSA population report for Connor Bedard Young Guns PSA 9 and PSA 10 grades

There have been just over 6,000 Bedard Young Guns that have been given a PSA 9 or PSA 10 grade since the product was released on Mar. 6, 2024. "Population" stands for the number of cards that were entered into the search engine and "Population Higher" stands for the number of cards that have a score that his higher than a PSA 9. Please note that PSA does not do half scoring for PSA 9 and above.

Advertisement

To look up the POP count for a PSA graded card, click [here](https://www.psacard.com/cert/) and enter the certification number on the right side of the PSA label.

Okay, we need to go back to the fact that over 6,000 Bedard Young Guns were issued PSA 9 or PSA 10 scores in the last 80 days. That means on average 75 new Bedard Young Guns with a score of PSA 9 or PSA 10 are hitting the market on a daily basis. Keep in mind that we are not accounting for the number of Bedard Young Guns that have a score of PSA 8.5 and below. Please also keep in mind that we are not referencing other grading companies like Beckett Card Grading Service (BGS) and Sportscard Guaranty Corporation (SGC).

There are well over 10,000 graded Bedard Young Guns in circulation. That is an incredible number for a product that was released nearly three months ago.

## What Does the POP Report Mean to You?

The POP report is your competition when it comes to buying and selling cards. A card with a high POP count, like the Bedard Young Guns, is commonly traded on a daily basis on eBay. It is easy to determine a value by averaging the last three to five sales.

Advertisement

Cards with high POP counts can be a double-edged sword for both buyer and seller. Sellers can attempt to hold out for their asking price for a hot card with a high POP count. Buyers can capitalize on the fact that many sellers are attempting to sell the same card. There is a reasonable chance a seller may consider taking 80%-90% of recent comps because there is high competition to sell the card.

![Connor Bedard Outburst Young Gun PSA 9](https://s.yimg.com/ny/api/res/1.2/71.I4ZIDLbDQPTOXyZEy.g--/YXBwaWQ9aGlnaGxhbmRlcjt3PTk2MDtoPTE2MDA7Y2Y9d2VicA--/https://media.zenfs.com/en/the_hockey_news_articles_331/48839f7bc13cf5e475dd34a09062322d)

Connor Bedard Outburst Young Gun PSA 9

The Young Guns Outburst parallel can be found in 1:60 Hobby packs. There are 50 different Young Guns cards. The odds of hitting a Bedard Young Guns Outburst parallel are 1:3,000 hobby packs. For comparison, the odds of hitting the base Bedard Young Guns is 1:100 Hobby packs.

As a result, the POP counts for the Bedard Outburst parallel are significantly lower.

![POP count for Connor Bedard Young Gun Outburst parallels ](https://s.yimg.com/ny/api/res/1.2/sB5m5SHlkNXjSriLFIao2Q--/YXBwaWQ9aGlnaGxhbmRlcjt3PTk2MDtoPTYxMDtjZj13ZWJw/https://media.zenfs.com/en/the_hockey_news_articles_331/53dc597b4b51c36f056613fc17a48556)

POP count for Connor Bedard Young Gun Outburst parallels

There are 5,959 less PSA 9 and PSA 10 Bedard Young Gun parallels than base Young Guns in circulation.

## Things to Factor

Remember, the POP count is your competition. Should you invest in cards with high or low POP counts? The answer is both. High POP count cards can be moved quickly as there will always be a buyer that is willing to pay near the full comp value of a card.

Low POP cards are nice to have because the seller can ultimately determine the value for the card. There are less sales to compare the card to. It is also likely that there are not many similar cards available for sale. The seller can take the approach of, "Yesterday's price is not today's price."

Happy hunting and collecting!

## What we know about the Knicks

You can say they’ve become the team Knicks fans hoped they would, but that doesn’t really cover it. Because I’m not sure even the most cockeyed, orange-and-blue-Kool-Aid-chugging optimist could’ve hoped for [*this*](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/article/its-a-chemistry-thing-behind-the-knicks-staggering-playoff-stretch-that-has-new-york-dreaming-big-215114546.html):

The Knicks entered the playoffs as a very good team: experienced and poised; 10-deep with players who can contribute; winners of 53 games (54, if you [count the NBA Cup final](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/article/the-knicks-won-the-nba-cup-without-a-hero-and-that-should-frighten-the-league-065205634.html)) with a [top-five net rating](https://www.nba.com/stats/teams/advanced?SeasonType=Regular%20Season&dir=A&sort=NET_RATING) and top-seven units on [both](https://www.nba.com/stats/teams/advanced?SeasonType=Regular%20Season&dir=A&sort=OFF_RATING) [sides](https://www.nba.com/stats/teams/advanced?SeasonType=Regular%20Season&dir=A&sort=DEF_RATING) of the ball. What they’ve been since [falling](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/article/nba-playoffs-2026-takeaways-msg-has-a-new-villain-in-cj-mccollum-after-hawks-shock-knicks-043907203.html) [down](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/article/knicks-cant-get-off-a-shot-against-hawks-in-wild-finish-of-game-3-as-cj-mccollum-lifts-atlanta-again-022315144.html) 2-1 against the [Hawks](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/teams/atlanta/) in Round 1, though, is awesome [in the 17th-century sense](https://www.etymonline.com/word/awesome) — a [titanic two-way terror](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/article/its-a-chemistry-thing-behind-the-knicks-staggering-playoff-stretch-that-has-new-york-dreaming-big-215114546.html) that has professionally and summarily chewed up and spat out its opposition to such a degree that it essentially had another All-Star break between the second and third rounds.

It was utter domination — a complete reversal of Game 6, a perfectly timed appearance of the best-case-scenario version of the roster that president of basketball operations Koby Altman put together, and a [31-point pummeling](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/live/cavaliers-dominate-game-7-to-advance-to-eastern-conference-finals-as-top-seeded-pistons-fall-flat-at-home-220000384.html) that sent the No. 1 seed home for the summer and propelled the Cavs onto New York and a chance to play for a spot in the NBA Finals.

The glass-half-empty take from surviving two nasty, gnarly, physical series just to make it to the conference finals — a first for Mitchell, Mobley and Allen, a return [nine years in the making](https://www.basketball-reference.com/teams/HOU/2018.html) for Harden, a round no Cleveland team without [LeBron James](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/players/3704/) has seen [in 35 years](https://www.basketball-reference.com/teams/CLE/1992.html) — is that the Cavs have taken a *lot* more hits than the Knicks have to get here. The glass-half-full view? They’ve now proven they can not only absorb those blows without buckling, but also fire back with enough force to win the fight.

That’s not something previous versions of the Cavs have been able to say; this one can. That toughness — plus a lot of offensive talent — has them four wins away from the Finals … and they’re going to need all of it against a Knicks team that’s absolutely surging right now.

## Matchup to watch

**Can the Cavs take away the Knicks’ Point KAT flow?**

You’ve likely heard it a million times by now: The Knicks’ decision to reorient their offense around Towns’ playmaking midway through the Hawks series has completely changed their trajectory.

Playing through Towns at the top of the floor and from the elbows has allowed the Knicks to exploit the unique matchup advantages he provides, as a 7-foot knockdown 3-point marksman who can see over the defense and drop the ball in a bucket to a cutting teammate, and who also has a handle deft enough to drive past lumbering defenders, enough bulk to muscle through smaller ones, and a touch silky enough to finish all types of shots from all types of angles through contact on his way to the rim.

Advertisement

Put your center on Towns, and New York can tap into the Brunson-Towns pick-and-roll, generating pick-and-pop triples for Towns or opportunities for Brunson to flame-broil an overmatched opposing big in space. Put a smaller defender on him, and the Knicks are becoming increasingly comfortable finding ways to play out of the cross-match, whether by bringing their own wings (Hart, Bridges, [OG Anunoby](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/players/5836/), McBride, even [Landry Shamet](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/players/6037/) and Jordan Clarkson) into the screening action to give Brunson a different vulnerable defender to try to exploit, or by letting Towns just pick out passes and dump the ball over the top of the smaller defender to cutters in [the motion offense that has been so successful of late](https://shaxnba.substack.com/p/magic-karl?r=5jsbb9&utm_campaign=post-expanded-share&utm_medium=web&triedRedirect=true).

That plan of attack annihilated [Atlanta](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/teams/atlanta/) and pummeled Philly, but Cleveland poses a different challenge. Mobley, the former Defensive Player of the Year, who served as Towns’ primary defender throughout the regular season, blends size, length, quickness, disruptive instincts and the ability to deploy them all in space at a rare level for a big man. And while most opponents with a top-flight defensive big to wield in this sort of matchup would have to sacrifice rim protection in doing so, the Cavs can let Mobley roam with Towns in space while still keeping Allen stationed close to the paint — especially if they slot him onto Hart, who shot 41.3% from 3-point range during the regular season, but who is battling [injuries to both of his hands](https://x.com/krispursiainen/status/2055330497853542584) and missed 27 of his first 34 3-point tries this postseason before going 4-for-6 in the Game 4 closeout of the Sixers.

If Hart’s unable to make the Cavs pay for [treating him like Ausar Thompson](https://x.com/HalfCourtHoops/status/2056176990877524092), and if Mobley’s able to stifle KAT’s high-post playmaking and pick-and-pop game, where will Brown turn? Is it a heavier dose of Brunson-ball — attempts to hunt Mitchell and Harden in the pick-and-roll or isolation, or to find the range against whichever wing defender Atkinson stations on him from the opening tip?

Advertisement

[Dean Wade](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/players/6256/) got the job in the February win, and acquitted himself well, but with his limited offensive game allowing the Pistons to all but ignore him on the offensive end, Atkinson decided to slide Strus into the starting five in Game 7. Strus held his own against Cunningham defensively and has the size, physicality and mentality to make Brunson work for every inch; Brunson’s postseason résumé is also littered with opponents who seemed like they could rough him up and take him out of his game, right up until he dropped 40 on them.

The Knicks have found plenty of playoff success these past couple of seasons behind that “give Brunson the ball and let God sort ’em out” style of approach, but it’s not what this team was built to be — not why they traded for Towns, not why they hired Brown, not why they put together the pieces they did. New York found the offensive identity it had been searching for against Atlanta and bludgeoned Philadelphia with it. If Cleveland can’t find a way to short-circuit it enough that the Knicks have to abandon it, the Cavs could find themselves on the receiving end of a similar offensive barrage, too.

## Key question

**How healthy is OG Anunoby, really?**

Anunoby got off to a roaring start to the 2026 postseason. The ninth-year forward averaged 21.4 points, 7.5 rebounds and more than six combined steals, blocks and deflections per game through New York’s first six playoff games, while shooting a scorching 67% on 2-pointers, 53.8% on 3-pointers and 81% from the foul line.

His off-ball cutting, physicality finishing in the paint and dead-eye shot-making helped fuel New York’s surging offense. His ability to seamlessly switch between defending quicker guards, like-sized power forwards and bigger centers helped unlock the most versatile and vicious version of the Knicks’ defense. Anunoby has never been an All-Star, but he was playing at an All- *NBA* level; there weren’t more than a handful of players performing as well as he was to start the postseason.

And then, late in Game 2 against Philly, he pulled up:

Anunoby missed the [final](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/article/knicks-og-anunoby-out-with-hamstring-strain-for-game-3-vs-76ers-still-considered-day-to-day-025035752.html) [two](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/breaking-news/article/og-anunoby-out-for-knicks-76ers-game-4-joel-embiid-to-start-for-philly-181951733.html) games of the Sixers series with a left hamstring strain — the type of injury that, depending on its severity, could keep a player on the sidelines for weeks. The initial reporting, though, suggested that Anunoby had [avoided a serious strain](https://x.com/SbondyNBA/status/2052470213892059282), and Knicks head coach Mike Brown consistently referred to him as “day-to-day.”

While New York waited for its opponent, Anunoby [returned to practice](https://x.com/Krisplashed/status/2055334740970873027), and [SNY’s Ian Begley](https://x.com/sny_knicks/status/2053909144134934992) and [ESPN’s Shams Charania](https://x.com/ShamsCharania/status/2054627018105393424) both reported that, barring any setbacks, he is [expected to be available for Game 1 of the conference finals](https://sports.yahoo.com/nba/breaking-news/article/knicks-og-anunoby-reportedly-expected-to-be-able-to-return-for-game-1-of-eastern-conference-finals-184432355.html). That’s good news for a Knicks team that will need him to knock down 3s, drive closeouts and finish through contact on the interior — all while serving as the primary defensive option against Mobley, who had a fantastic close to Round 2 as a finisher and secondary playmaker, and to be able to switch onto either Mitchell or Harden to stifle a Cavs pick-and-roll game that can be one of the NBA’s very best when it’s humming.

But when he returns to the court after nearly two weeks away from live game action, will Anunoby be able to immediately rediscover the phenomenal shot-making rhythm he was in before the injury? Will he be able to move as quickly and fluidly on defense, and play with the same level of confidence and physicality? On top of that, hamstrings are notoriously fickle things; even with the additional rest afforded by the Sixers sweep, might Anunoby be risking re-injury, and a longer potential layoff, by coming back for the start of the series?

The best version of the Knicks features Anunoby finishing offensive possessions and blowing up defensive ones. If he comes back looking like the two-way monster who was dominating Atlanta and Philadelphia, their chances of advancing to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999 improve dramatically. If he’s limited, though, New York becomes less potent and more vulnerable on both ends of the floor.

## Prediction: Knicks in 6

If the version of the Cavaliers we saw in Detroit on Sunday night is the one that shows up for the next two weeks, Cleveland can absolutely make it to the Finals. But with New York having such a massive rest advantage, with less than 48 hours between the end of Game 7 and the start of Game 1, with the Cavs coming off consecutive grueling seven-game series with *five* players (Harden, Mitchell, Mobley, Allen and Strus) having [played more postseason minutes](https://www.nba.com/stats/team/1610612739/players-traditional?PerMode=Totals&dir=D&sort=MIN) than [any member of the Knicks](https://www.nba.com/stats/team/1610612752/players-traditional?PerMode=Totals&dir=D&sort=MIN), and with the games coming every other day without an opportunity to catch a breather, I think it’s more likely the Cavs run out of gas than a Knicks team that’s been firing on all cylinders sputters through the finish line.

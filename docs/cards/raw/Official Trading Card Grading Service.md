## Grading

PSA is the largest and most respected third-party authentication and grading company in the world for trading cards and memorabilia.

Authentication is the process of verifying the originality or genuineness of a trading card. Grading is assessing the quality and condition of a trading card using [PSA’s 10-point grading scale](https://www.psacard.com/resources/gradingstandards#cards). Grading can only take place after a trading card has been deemed authentic.

Whether vintage, modern or TCG, PSA authenticates and grades cards throughout the hobby. Learn more about our grading process, insightful content and collecting resources below.

## Trading Card Authentication & Grading Process

Step 1

## Authentication

A series of PSA graders review your cards for authenticity. If genuine, PSA looks for evidence of doctoring, such as re-coloring or trimming.

Step 2

## Grading

If your cards pass authentication, PSA grades the condition of each card on a [scale of 1-10](https://www.psacard.com/resources/gradingstandards#cards), with 10 being best.

Step 3

## Encapsulation

PSA encapsulates each card in its own sonically sealed, [tamper-evident case](https://www.psacard.com/security). The card's grade and [certification number](https://www.psacard.com/cert) are displayed on the PSA LightHouse™ label.

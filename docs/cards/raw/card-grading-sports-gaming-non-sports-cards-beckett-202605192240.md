# What Is Card Grading?

Card grading is the process of having a professional examine your card and assign it a numerical score based on its physical condition. Every card is assessed across four criteria — centering, corners, edges, and surface — and sealed in a tamper-evident holder that displays the grade. For collectors, a grade removes the guesswork from buying and selling: you know exactly what you have, and so does everyone else. For the hobby, it's the difference between opinion and fact.

## EXPLORE SUBGRADES

Cards are graded on four key categories: centering, corners, edges and surface.

Beckett Grading is the first to use this unique system which assures greater accuracy and consistency in grading.

![2003-04 TOPPS CHROME #111 Lebron James Card](https://www.beckett.com/_next/image?url=%2F_next%2Fstatic%2Fmedia%2FLebronJames.d256eed6.png&w=640&q=75)

##### CORNERS

The corners of a trading card are one of the most critical factors in determining the card's grade. The card should be free of dings, creases, bends, or discoloration. Cards with these flaws will receive a lower grade.

##### CENTERING

Centering refers to the alignment of the card within its borders. A card with perfect centering has even borders on all sides. If a card is off-center, it can affect the overall appearance of the card and lower its grade.

##### Half-Point Grades

Please note that Beckett Grading Services provides final grades in half-point increments (i.e., 10, 9.5, 9, 8.5 etc.). Cards that are assigned a grade with a half-point increment typically share characteristics from both the level above and the level below the actual grade given.
